eBay Reseller Automation

eBay Automation for Tennessee Resellers

Manage more inventory in less time. Built for estate sale operators, antique dealers, and collectibles resellers in Tennessee and the surrounding region.

If you're running a reselling operation in Tennessee — estate sales, antique markets, flea markets, storage unit finds — you already know the math problem. The more inventory you have, the more time you spend listing. And the more time you spend listing, the less time you have to source. The business eats itself.

Manual eBay listing is one of the most recoverable drains in a reseller's operation. It's repetitive, it's slow, and most of the steps don't actually require a human making decisions. They require someone typing the same things over and over. That's what automation is built for.

What manual listing is actually costing you

Most resellers don't track the time they spend listing per item. If you did, you'd find a number between 8 and 20 minutes per item depending on your category and condition grading process. At the low end, that's 8 hours to list 60 items. At the high end, it's a full 20-hour work week to get 60 items live.

That's not including:

  • Pulling pricing comps manually on sold listings
  • Writing condition descriptions from scratch each time
  • Resizing and uploading photos
  • Tracking what's sold, what's sitting, what needs a price drop

In a real estate sale operation, you might buy 200–400 items in a single weekend. Getting those listed in any reasonable timeframe with manual processes is the bottleneck that caps your revenue — not inventory acquisition, not sourcing, not sales volume. The listing queue.

What we automate

We've built and run an eBay automation stack for a real reselling operation currently managing 565 active listings. The system handles:

Listing creation

AI-generated titles and descriptions based on photo analysis and item identification. The system identifies the item, determines the category, writes a complete listing including condition notes — ready for review and one-click publish. What used to take 12 minutes takes 90 seconds.

Pricing research

Automated comp pulling from eBay sold listings. The system queries comparable sold items, filters by condition and recency, and outputs a recommended price range with a suggested list price. No more manually searching "what did this sell for" on every item.

Inventory tracking

A live inventory tracker that knows what's listed, what's sold, what's sitting over 30/60/90 days, and what needs a price adjustment. Generates a daily report you can act on in under 10 minutes instead of an hour of manual review.

Item identification for collectibles

For vintage items, antiques, and collectibles — which are common in Tennessee estate sales — we run a six-track identification engine that uses multiple AI models to consensus-identify an item from photos. This is particularly useful for items where you know it's worth money but aren't sure exactly what it is or what category it belongs in.

The Tennessee and Midwest corridor

Tennessee resellers work a specific geographic and cultural inventory. Estate sales in the Nashville, Knoxville, Memphis, and Chattanooga markets consistently produce certain categories: mid-century furniture, Depression glass, vintage textiles, old tools and hardware, country music memorabilia, Civil War and military collectibles, Southern pottery, and a constant stream of general household items.

The same patterns hold across the TN/IL corridor — Springfield, Peoria, Decatur — where estate liquidation events run year-round and produce high volume for resellers who can process fast enough to compete.

The problem isn't finding inventory. It's processing it fast enough to stay ahead. Automation closes that gap.

What this looks like in practice

A typical workflow after setup:

  • You come home from an estate sale with 80 items
  • You photograph each item (phone photos are fine, no studio required)
  • You batch-upload to the system
  • The system identifies each item, writes listings, pulls comps, and queues everything for review
  • You review and approve — or edit anything that needs adjustment
  • All 80 items are live in the time it used to take to list 12

The review step is important — we don't remove human judgment, we just remove the mechanical parts that don't require it. You still decide what price you're comfortable with. You still catch the things the AI misses. But you're making 80 decisions in 45 minutes instead of spending 16 hours typing.

Real numbers from the system we run

We're not describing a hypothetical. Here's what's running in our own operation:

  • Average time per new listing: under 3 minutes including review

These are real numbers from a real operation. They'll vary based on your inventory type, volume, and how much customization you need. But they're in the right ballpark for what's achievable.
